A compact full-format mock that mirrors the digital SAT in miniature: one **Reading & Writing** part (11 questions) and one **Math** part (11 questions). Do it **timed and in one sitting** to practice pacing.

> **Timing:** Reading & Writing — **13 minutes** (≈71 s/question). Math — **17 minutes** (≈95 s/question). No phone, scratch paper for math, calculator allowed for math.

---

## Part 1 — Reading & Writing (13 min)

**1.** "The recipe calls for fresh basil; ______, dried basil can be used in a pinch."
(A) therefore  (B) however  (C) for example  (D) similarly

**2.** "The committee, after weeks of debate, ______ a final decision yesterday."
(A) reach  (B) reaches  (C) reached  (D) reaching

**3.** Which choice best states the main idea? *A passage explains that octopuses can change skin color and texture within seconds to blend into many backgrounds.*
(A) Octopuses are the smartest sea animals.
(B) Octopuses use rapid skin changes for effective camouflage.
(C) Octopuses prefer coral reefs.
(D) Octopuses change color only at night.

**4.** "______ the bridge in 1937, the engineers used a then-novel cable design."
(A) Building  (B) To build  (C) Having built  (D) When building

**5.** "The author's tone is best described as ______: she praises the invention while noting its risks."
(A) dismissive  (B) measured  (C) hostile  (D) nostalgic

**6.** "Maria enjoys three hobbies: painting, hiking, ______ photography."
(A) and  (B) , and  (C) ; and  (D) and also

**7.** Goal: *emphasize the result of the policy.* Best choice:
(A) "The policy was introduced in 2020."
(B) "As a result of the policy, recycling rates doubled."
(C) "The policy was debated for months."
(D) "Some residents disliked the policy."

**8.** "Each of the planets in the model ______ to scale."
(A) are  (B) is  (C) were  (D) have been

**9.** "The novel's structure—three interlocking timelines ______ rewards careful re-reading."
(A) ,  (B) —  (C) :  (D) (no punctuation)

**10.** "Critics first called the painting careless; later analysis, however, revealed ______"
(A) it was indeed careless.
(B) a deliberate, highly planned composition.
(C) the artist disliked it.
(D) nothing of interest.

**11.** Text 1 claims social media mainly harms teen focus. Text 2 reports that moderate, purposeful use improves peer connection. The author of Text 2 would most likely say Text 1:
(A) is fully correct.
(B) overgeneralizes by ignoring how the platform is used.
(C) underestimates the harm.
(D) is about adults, not teens.

## Part 2 — Math (17 min)

**12.** If $4(x - 2) = 20$, what is $x$?

**13.** A line passes through $(0, -3)$ and $(2, 1)$. What is its slope?

**14.** Solve $x^2 - 7x + 10 = 0$.

**15.** If $3^{x} = 81$, what is $x$?

**16.** A shirt costs \$40 after a 20% discount. What was the original price?

**17.** The mean of $6, 9, x, 12$ is $10$. Find $x$.

**18.** A right triangle has legs 7 and 24. Find the hypotenuse.

**19.** A circle has area $49\pi$. What is its circumference?

**20.** If $f(x) = 2x^2 - 3$, what is $f(4)$?

**21.** A bag has 5 red and 15 blue marbles. What is the probability a random marble is red?

**22.** Line $\ell$ has equation $y = -\tfrac{1}{2}x + 4$. What is the slope of a line **perpendicular** to $\ell$?

## Answer Key

**Reading & Writing**

1. **B** — contrast (fresh vs dried substitute) → *however*.
2. **C** — past action ("yesterday") and singular subject → *reached*.
3. **B** — ties skin change to camouflage; others overreach.
4. **D** — "When building… in 1937" correctly modifies *the engineers*.
5. **B** — praise + noting risks = balanced → *measured*.
6. **A** — a list already using commas needs only *and* before the last item here (painting, hiking, and photography).
7. **B** — only (B) states a result.
8. **B** — subject *Each* (singular) → *is*.
9. **B** — dash pair sets off the nonessential description; match the opening dash.
10. **B** — "however… revealed" signals a contrast with "careless" → deliberate composition.
11. **B** — Text 2 stresses *how* it is used → Text 1 overgeneralizes.

**Math**

12. **$x = 7$** — $x - 2 = 5$.
13. **$2$** — $\frac{1-(-3)}{2-0} = \frac{4}{2}$.
14. **$x = 2$ or $5$** — $(x-2)(x-5)=0$.
15. **$x = 4$** — $81 = 3^4$.
16. **\$50** — $40 = 0.8p \Rightarrow p = 50$.
17. **$x = 13$** — $6+9+x+12 = 40 \Rightarrow x = 13$.
18. **$25$** — $\sqrt{49+576} = \sqrt{625}$.
19. **$14\pi$** — $r = 7$; $2\pi(7)$.
20. **$29$** — $2(16) - 3$.
21. **$\tfrac{5}{20} = \tfrac14 = 0.25$**.
22. **$2$** — perpendicular slope is the negative reciprocal of $-\tfrac12$.

**Score check (this mini test):** count correct out of 22. ≥19 strong, 14–18 solid, <14 → review the missed domains in the Knowledge Points notes.
