The Digital SAT Math section has **44 questions in 70 minutes** (two modules of 22 questions). About 75% are multiple choice and 25% are student-produced response (grid-in). A formula **reference sheet** is provided, and a Desmos calculator is built in — but setting up the equation correctly is what saves time.

This note is organized by the **four official domains**. For each: the key ideas, then practice problems with worked solutions.

---

## 1. Algebra

**Key ideas**

- Slope-intercept form: $y = mx + b$, where $m$ is slope and $b$ is the $y$-intercept.
- Slope between two points: $m = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$.
- Parallel lines have equal slopes; perpendicular slopes multiply to $-1$.
- A system $\begin{cases}a_1x+b_1y=c_1\\ a_2x+b_2y=c_2\end{cases}$ has **no solution** when the lines are parallel (same slope, different intercept) and **infinitely many** when they are identical.

**Practice 1.1.** If $3x - 5 = 2x + 7$, what is the value of $x$?

> **Solution.** $3x - 2x = 7 + 5 \Rightarrow x = 12.$

**Practice 1.2.** A line passes through $(2, 1)$ and $(6, 9)$. What is its equation?

> **Solution.** Slope $m = \dfrac{9-1}{6-2} = 2$. Using $y - 1 = 2(x - 2)$ gives $y = 2x - 3.$

**Practice 1.3.** For what value of $k$ does the system have **no solution**?
$$y = 4x + 1, \qquad y = kx - 3$$

> **Solution.** No solution means parallel but not identical lines: equal slopes, different intercepts. So $k = 4.$

## 2. Advanced Math

**Key ideas**

- Quadratic formula: $x = \dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ for $ax^2+bx+c=0$.
- Discriminant $b^2 - 4ac$: positive → two real roots; zero → one; negative → none.
- Vertex form $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$ has vertex $(h,k)$; the vertex $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a}$.
- Exponent rules: $x^a x^b = x^{a+b}$, $\dfrac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}$, $(x^a)^b = x^{ab}$, $x^{-a} = \dfrac{1}{x^a}$, $x^{1/2} = \sqrt{x}$.

**Practice 2.1.** Solve $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$.

> **Solution.** Factor: $(x-2)(x-3)=0 \Rightarrow x = 2$ or $x = 3.$

**Practice 2.2.** The function $f(x) = x^2 - 6x + 11$ has its minimum at what value of $x$?

> **Solution.** $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a} = -\dfrac{-6}{2(1)} = 3.$ (Minimum value $f(3)=2$.)

**Practice 2.3.** If $2^{x} = 32$, what is $x$?

> **Solution.** $32 = 2^5$, so $x = 5.$

## 3. Problem-Solving and Data Analysis

**Key ideas**

- Percent change: $\dfrac{\text{new} - \text{old}}{\text{old}} \times 100\%$.
- A $p\%$ increase multiplies by $\left(1 + \dfrac{p}{100}\right)$; a decrease by $\left(1 - \dfrac{p}{100}\right)$.
- Mean $= \dfrac{\text{sum}}{\text{count}}$; median = middle value; range = max − min. Larger spread → larger standard deviation.
- Probability $= \dfrac{\text{favorable outcomes}}{\text{total outcomes}}$.

**Practice 3.1.** A jacket priced at \$80 is discounted by 25%. What is the sale price?

> **Solution.** $80 \times (1 - 0.25) = 80 \times 0.75 = \$60.$

**Practice 3.2.** A car travels 150 miles in 2.5 hours. At this rate, how far in 4 hours?

> **Solution.** Rate $= \dfrac{150}{2.5} = 60$ mph. Distance $= 60 \times 4 = 240$ miles.

**Practice 3.3.** The data set is $4, 7, 7, 10, 12$. Find the mean and median.

> **Solution.** Mean $= \dfrac{4+7+7+10+12}{5} = \dfrac{40}{5} = 8$. Median = middle value $= 7.$

## 4. Geometry and Trigonometry

**Key ideas (many are on the reference sheet)**

- Circle: area $= \pi r^2$, circumference $= 2\pi r$.
- Pythagorean theorem: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.
- Special right triangles: $45\text{-}45\text{-}90 \to$ sides $x, x, x\sqrt{2}$; $\;30\text{-}60\text{-}90 \to x, x\sqrt{3}, 2x$.
- Trig (right triangle): $\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}$, $\cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}$, $\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}$.
- Angles on a line sum to $180^\circ$; angles in a triangle sum to $180^\circ$.

**Practice 4.1.** A right triangle has legs 6 and 8. What is the hypotenuse?

> **Solution.** $c = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36+64} = \sqrt{100} = 10.$

**Practice 4.2.** A circle has area $36\pi$. What is its circumference?

> **Solution.** $\pi r^2 = 36\pi \Rightarrow r = 6$. Circumference $= 2\pi(6) = 12\pi.$

**Practice 4.3.** In a right triangle, the angle $\theta$ has opposite side 3 and hypotenuse 5. Find $\sin\theta$ and $\cos\theta$.

> **Solution.** $\sin\theta = \dfrac{3}{5}$. The adjacent side $= \sqrt{5^2 - 3^2} = 4$, so $\cos\theta = \dfrac{4}{5}.$
