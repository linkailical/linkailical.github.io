## A. Standard English Conventions (grammar)

**1.** "The museum opened a new wing ______ it now displays over two hundred sculptures."
(A) , and  (B) ,  (C) and  (D) ; and

**2.** "Each of the students ______ responsible for one section of the report."
(A) are  (B) is  (C) were  (D) have been

**3.** "______ the long delay, the passengers remained calm."
(A) Despite  (B) Because  (C) Therefore  (D) However

**4.** "The scientist recorded her data carefully, ______ she could repeat the experiment later."
(A) so that  (B) however  (C) in contrast  (D) for example

**5.** "Founded in 1885, ______ has expanded to five campuses."
(A) the university's growth  (B) there are five campuses  (C) the university  (D) growth at the university

**6.** "My favorite authors—Austen, Orwell, and Morrison ______ all shaped how I think about writing."
(A) ,  (B) —  (C) :  (D) ;

## B. Expression of Ideas

**7.** "Solar panels were expensive a decade ago. ______, prices have fallen by more than half."
(A) Therefore  (B) Since then  (C) For example  (D) Likewise

**8.** Goal: *emphasize the contrast between the two methods.*
(A) "Method A and Method B both use heat."
(B) "Method A is fast, whereas Method B is slow but more accurate."
(C) "Method B was developed after Method A."
(D) "Both methods are widely used."

**9.** Goal: *introduce the topic to readers unfamiliar with it.*
(A) "As is well known, photosynthesis is light-dependent."
(B) "Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light into chemical energy."
(C) "The Calvin cycle's enzymes are temperature-sensitive."
(D) "Researchers debate the rate-limiting step."

## C. Craft and Structure

**10.** "The evidence was ______: it pointed clearly to a single conclusion."
(A) ambiguous  (B) conclusive  (C) trivial  (D) misleading

**11.** "Her tone grew ______; she no longer hid her irritation."
(A) cheerful  (B) neutral  (C) sharp  (D) hesitant

**12.** A paragraph gives three everyday examples before stating a scientific principle. The examples most likely serve to:
(A) disprove the principle.
(B) make the abstract principle easier to grasp.
(C) show the principle is rarely true.
(D) replace the need for a definition.

**13.** Text 1 argues city parks improve public health. Text 2 presents data that park use is highest among already-healthy residents. The author of Text 2 would most likely respond that Text 1:
(A) overstates a causal benefit that the data complicate.
(B) is entirely correct.
(C) underestimates the cost of parks.
(D) ignores the beauty of parks.

## D. Information and Ideas

**14.** A passage states a frog species absorbs water through its skin and dies quickly in dry air. Which best states the main idea?
(A) The frog needs a moist environment to survive.
(B) The frog never drinks with its mouth, so it is unusual.
(C) Dry air is harmful to all amphibians.
(D) The frog's skin is thicker than most.

**15.** "The 1920 census recorded rapid urban growth; ______"
(A) most citizens preferred rural life.
(B) cities were clearly losing population.
(C) more Americans were moving into cities than before.
(D) the census was inaccurate.

**16.** A chart shows test scores rose each year from 2019 to 2023. Which claim does the chart support?
(A) Scores declined after 2021.
(B) Scores increased steadily across the period.
(C) Scores stayed the same.
(D) Scores peaked in 2019.

## Answer Key

1. **A** — two independent clauses → comma + *and*. (B) is a comma splice; (D) misuses the semicolon with *and*.
2. **B** — subject is *Each* (singular) → *is*.
3. **A** — contrast between delay and calm → *Despite*.
4. **A** — purpose ("so she could repeat") → *so that*.
5. **C** — the modifier "Founded in 1885" must describe *the university*.
6. **B** — the dash pair sets off the nonessential list; match the opening dash.
7. **B** — time relationship ("a decade ago" → now) → *Since then*.
8. **B** — only (B) states a contrast.
9. **B** — a plain definition suits unfamiliar readers; (A)/(C)/(D) assume prior knowledge.
10. **B** — "pointed clearly to a single conclusion" = *conclusive*.
11. **C** — "no longer hid her irritation" → *sharp*.
12. **B** — examples make the abstract principle concrete.
13. **A** — Text 2's data challenge a simple causal claim.
14. **A** — both facts point to needing moisture; others overreach.
15. **C** — "rapid urban growth" logically completes with more people moving to cities.
16. **B** — rose each year → steady increase.
